From the disfigured stone structures of the Paleolithic period to the virtual Pokemon that we are running behind today, the idea of ascribing meaning to an inanimate object has always been an intriguing element of the human mind. This romanticism of objects has been explored by philosophers and poets alike - the corn is “orient and immortal wheat” in Wordsworth’s Daffodils, a city is a tale of its past in Calvino’s Invisible Cities and so on. Objects have never been mere objects but ideas, signs, feelings and aspirations. Even today, as we transition from believing in a deity to worshipping the omniscient algorithms, we carry with us the same fetish towards objects, physical or abstract, existent or non-existent.

Totem is a digital archive of modern, day-to-day objects or entities and the ritualized practices associated with them in the contemporary age of virtuality and digitization. The archive is just as much a collection of experiences and stories as it is of objects in their digital form.
